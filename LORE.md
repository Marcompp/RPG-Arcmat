# Arcmat — Lore Reference

Quick-reference for Claude Code when writing in-world text: event dialogue, item descriptions, location flavor, NPC lines. All content is derived from the JSON databases; flag any contradiction you find.

---

## World Overview

- **Setting:** The continent of Rozar, a far vaster world, with only a small slice of it being visited during the game.
- **Tone:** Light-hearted, almost absurdist at times, without breaking the world. Mostly grounded. Sensory detail over spectacle (smell of bread, sound of ropes groaning, warmth of crystals). Events can be funny, tense, or eerie — even cartoonish. The world feels lived-in.
- **Magic origin:** All magic traces back to the **Arcmat Ruins** — an impossibly precise ancient structure of black geometry and violet energy. Mages and scholars of all stripes seek to understand its secrets.
- **Premise** The player plays one of four adventurers who caught wind of a treasure in the Arcmat Ruins. Their journey starts at the Apple Woods, and they travel west to east until they reach the Arcmat Ruins.

## Protagonists

- **Agnes** — Fencer. Sharp, competitive, arrogant, suspicious of strangers. Female. An adventurer of noble birth. Rival with Borin; views Danil as a lowlife thief. From Bertunia. Redheaded. Uses a Foil; wears a Corset; carries the Duelist Glove trinket. 
When encountered as a boss, uses a Rapier and the Duelist class, representing her being a little ways into his journey.
- **Borin** — Warrior, former woodcutter. Grounded, protective, brusque. Male. Has black hair, a big physique and a square face. A career adventurer. Older brother of Chala; rival of Agnes. Uses a Hatchet; wears a Tanktop; carries the Woodcutter's Bandana trinket.
When encountered as a Boss, uses a Poleaxe and the Woodcutter class, representing him being little ways into his journey.
- **Chala** — Mage. Borin's younger sister. Female. Powerful, emotional, young, inexperienced, a bit naive and unserious, has a crush on Danil. Blonde. A talented student of magic, who nevertheless seeks adventure. Uses a Wooden Rod; wears a Uniform; carries the Resonant Badge trinket. Knows Thunder and Poison magic.
- **Danil** — Rogue. Agile, morally ambiguous, young, lighthearted and suave. Views Agnes as a threat; close to Chala, slightly afraid of Borin. Uses a Sling; wears a Tanktop; carries the Hidden Blade trinket.

## Regions (Story Order)

### 1. Apple Woods
A sunny forest outside of Bertunia, known for its apple trees.

Worn dirt roads through deep forest, apple orchards, a swift cold river. First dangers are mundane — bandits, slimes. The true threat is the Werewolf: a former hunter who lost himself to his curse.

Key locations: Road, River, Bridge (moss-covered), Clearing, Spring, Lake, Cabin (the Apple Witch's and/or the Werewolf's), Fork in the Road (faded signpost).

### 2. Caves of Light
Underground caverns west of Bertunia, under the Sundown Plateau, with luminous walls that shimmer with yellow light and humming blue crystals crystals that radiate warmth and faint energy.

The mysterious power of the cavern has attracted the attention of fanatics who style themselves The Cult of Light; their rituals meant to revere the Light that lives in the caves.

Key locations: Tunnel (shimmering crystals), Grotto, Cavern (light scatters like stars), Spring (luminous water), Altar (offering basin), Cult Sanctum (cloaked figures, carved symbols pulsing with dim blue energy).

### 3. Sundown Plateau
A vast, plateau west of Bertunia, on the East side of Mt. Legory, and right on top of the Caves of Light.
Historically used as a graveyard, the dead are known to rise here, perhaps because of the Caves of Light's power radiating underground. This has made it a hotspot for necromancers. 
Ancient catacombs lie beneath a mausoleum entrance.

The **Gilded Hollow Circus** (Gilded Hollow Circus — All Welcome) travels here: uncany and somewhat sinister, with striped red and purple tents, a slightly-off-key calliope, caged animals, and sequined performers. In truth, it is run by demons, and once inside they do not let their audience leave.

Key locations: Graveyard (Maren Holt, Aldric Voss memorials), Altar (crumbling, offerings now dust), Mausoleum, Catacomb Tunnel, Forked Tunnel, Sanctum (pillars etched in old symbols, floor cracks glow), **Lantern's Reach**.

### 4. Mount Legory
The southernmost of the Whitewing Mountains, notable for being located pretty far from the nearest peak.
It demarcates the western border of the petty kingdom of Cidonia.
The Sundown Plateau lies to east, the Magical Academy of Arcward is located on the western foothills.

Snow-covered, biting wind, thin air. The mountain demands respect. Pegasus Knights patrol the ridge; climbers leave cairns of stones and tokens (prayer beads, coins, cloth strips). A Great Roc nests at the summit.

Key locations: Ascent Path, Mountain Cave (abandoned camp inside), Ridge (steep drops on both sides), Archway (stone arch with old climbers' carvings), Summit (Great Roc's nest, collected weapons, views under clouds).

### 5. Core Cavern
The volcanic heart beneath Mount Legory. Churning magma, obsidian chambers, blinding heat. Home to Wyverns, fire Wisps, and the territorial Lizardmen civilization. The Infrit guards the deepest chamber.

Key locations: Cracked Descent, Magma Flow, Basalt Bridge (narrow, cracked), Obsidian Chamber (distorted reflections), Ash Field (unstable ground), Core Chamber (churning magma, blinding light), **Lizal-Town**.

### 6. Lost Swamp
West of Arcward.
A cursed wetland of thick white fog where many have vanished. Ancient half-sunken shrines suggest a civilization that didn't survive. The King of the Swamp holds dominion over everything here.

The dull white fogs of the Lost Swamp make visibility very poor. It is said that they are formed of the Lost Souls of those who were lost in the swamp.

Key locations: Foggy Marsh, Sunken Path (old submerged stones), Twisted Trees (impossible angles, grasping roots), Still Water, Whispering Reeds (sounds like voices), Lost Shrine (carvings worn beyond recognition — "it feels like this place should not be found"), Endless Hollow (total fog, total silence), Half-Sunken Boat, **Arcward**, **Arcmat Ruins**, **Mugell Town**.

### 7. Arcmat Ruins
Impossibly ancient ruins that lie deep in the Lost Swamp.

Known as the origin of all magic, little else is known about the civilization that built it.
A topic that fascinated researchers and mages for millenia.

Carlon the Mathemagician is said to have chosen the location of Arcward because of its proximity to the ruins. He's also said to have vanished in the Lost Swamp during one of his research trips.

Precise black geometric structures with unnatural symmetry. Onyx cubes stacked impossibly. Violet energy arcs between stones. The swamp fog stops at the threshold — a clean border between worlds. Glyphs shift when unobserved. Gravity is uncertain in the floating atrium.

Key locations: Outer Threshold, Fractured Hall (suspended cubes held by violet energy threads), Glyph Corridor (shifting inscriptions), Mechanism Chamber (interlocking parts, energy flowing like blood), Floating Atrium (drifting stones), Arcane Conduit (concentrated violet energy, unstable), Scriptorium (dense shifting indecipherable script), Core Nexus (pulsing geometric heart of the ruins).

## Factions & Organizations

- **The Cult of Light** — Led by Mother Orielli, operating from the Caves of Light. Fanatics who worship the power of the Light that suffuses the Caves of Light — territorial, they patrol, recruit, and perform rituals. Carry Prayer Beads and Sacrificial Knives; wear white robes.
- **Pegasus Knights** — Mounted female warriors of the Whitewing Mountains. Organized military unit in standardized Whitewing Armor with Winged Spears. Disciplined and pragmatic; they are mostly mercenaries, who offer their flying utility to foreign armies, to bring back money to their bare home; reward courtesy with gold, respond to aggression with force.
- **Lizardmen** — More properly named Lizals. Intelligent humanoids, often considered simple monsters. They have a hidden civilization deep in the Core Cavern. Territorial, not inherently hostile. Wear Lizal Armor; wield Falchions. Their town, Lizal-Town, sits near the cavern.
- **Adventurer's Guild** — Institutional presence in Lantern's Reach. Sells training scrolls; implies adventuring is a recognized, organized profession.
- **Magical Academy of Arcward** — Educational institution near Lost Swamp, at the western foothills of Mount Legory. Founded by Archmage Carlon the Mathemagician. One of the premier institutions of learning in the continent. Produces scholars and mages. Guarded by militiamen in Arcward Armor. Students are usually enrolled at fifteen and graduate in five years. The core disciplines taught include Mathematics, History, Administration, Spellcraft, Runecraft, Alchemy and Combat

## Bestiary (Lore Notes)

**Wildlife**
- **Slime** — Gelatinous water creatures; naturally regenerate; the most basic magical organism in the world.
- **Bitefish** — Aggressive river predators; Apple River is more dangerous than it looks.
- **Bear** — Rare apex predator of Apple Woods. Avoids humans unless cornered.
- **Vampire Bat** — Bloodsucker; dwells in Caves of Light. Drinks blood.
- **Sparkfish** — Electrified fish native to the Caves of Light.
- **Chimera** — Hybrid amphibious beast; part lion, part salamander, part goat, with a snake tail. The most powerful beast in Caves of Light.
- **Mangy Wolf** — Hungry skinny wolves native to the Sundown Plateau.
- **Bald Eagle** — Mount Legory's natural hunter. Territorial.
- **Great Roc** — Massive legendary bird nesting at Mount Legory's summit. Has feathers like blades of stone. Has collected weapons in its nest over decades. Tolerates human presence unless threatened.
- **Wyvern** — Draconic beast of Core Cavern.
- **Ent** — Ancient tree-being of Lost Swamp. Primordial and slow. The King of the Swamp is an impossibily ancient Ent.
- **King of the Swamp** — Ancient Ent who sleeps in the Lost Swamp. Wields Ent Club.

**Humanoids**
- **Bandits / Bandit Brothers** — Human criminals who work in organized ambushes through Apple Woods. Can sometimes be persuaded.
- **Renegades** — Bertunian criminals who hide out in the Caves of Light. Can sometimes be persuaded.
- **Troll** — Bulky, hardy humanoid who once, whose civilization once ruled the continent. Now, scattered and territorial, with little in the way of civilization, to the point of being seen as little more than monsters by humans.
- **Cultists / Cultist Patrol** — True believers of Mother Orielli's light cult. Fanatic followers who worship the Light; they actively recruit.
- **Pegasus Knight** — Soldiers and mercenaries, not monsters. Engage only if provoked or disrespected.
- **Frost Fairy** — An elemental fairy, small enough to fit in a person's hand. Sleeps in snowdrifts on Mount Legory; a pure ice spellcaster.
- **Treasure Hunter** — Fellow adventurers encountered as enemies in Arcmat Ruins. Well-equipped, cunning, competitive.
- **Lizardman** — Sentient humanoids with lizard-features Core Cavern inhabitant. Armed and territorial.
- **Gorgon** — Ophidian monster of Lost Swamp; petrifying eye, venomous bite. Fully sentient, but usually taken as monsters. Has snakes for hair, and a serpentine lower body, and a single large eye.
- **Wizard** (Ruins) — Magic users who seek to study the Arcmat Ruins and lost themselves in the knowledge. Masters all elemental magic. Dangerous and deliberate.
- **Infrit** — Demonic fire elemental; guards the Core Caverns. Very angry. Speaks in all caps.
- **Icelady** — Ice elemental demon of Mount Legory. High intelligence; wields Frost Staff. Vain and cruel.

**Undead**
- **Skeleton** — Animated bones; wields a Scimitar; basic undead footsoldier.
- **Zombie** — Reanimated corpse; relentless, high HP, slow.
- **Ghost / Restless Spirit** — Bound spirits; some can be communicated with; some only want to move on.
- **Abomination** — A mass stitched from multiple corpses; the most extreme product of necromancy; guards the deepest catacomb chamber.
- **Wisp** — Fire spirit of Core Cavern; technically a type of ghost, but its fire nature takes precedence.
- **Lost Souls** — Ethereal spirits haunting the Lost Swamp; mindless silently screaming apparitions that make up the dull white fogs of the Lost Swamp, said to be all that remains of those lost in the Lost Swamp.

**Supernatural**
- **Gargoyle** — Stone guardian of Arcmat Ruins; animated by the ruins' residual magic.
- **Werewolf** — A transformed hunter. Still leaves disturbances (things moved, not stolen) at his old cabin — he remembers.

**Named Bosses**
- **Mother Orielli** — Cult Leader. Offers blessings before combat; fights with Sacrificial Knife, Light and Heal magic.
- **Necromancer** — A mad mage who seeks power through the command of undeath. Wields Skull Staff; wears Necromancer Robes.
- **Carlon, the Mathemagician** — Final boss of Arcmat Ruins. Light element, Level 20. Wields a Geometry Staff; wears Archmage Robes. His signature skill is "Integer" — an application of "Mathemagic", a school of magic, said to be the first, of which he is quite possibly the greatest living user; geometric magical calculation made lethal. A historical Archmage who sought the lost knowledge of the ruins and lost himself in his studies. Cold, academic. Commands Earth, Light, and Thunder spells.

## Elemental System (Lore Level)

Mages can be attuned to up to five elements. This means that any one person can only ever cast spells of five different elements at most. For gameplay reasons, the difficulty of attuning to a new element is waved off, but the player will only be able to use 4 spells at most.

The base element of a being is determined by what they 'are', not necessarily what they can muster. IE: a fish is Water elemented by default, even if it can breathe fire. Off-element abilities are expected. Plus sometimes a spell or ability sourced from one element can have an effect that is of another. IE: Shiver is meant to represent the weaponisation of ghostliness, the shiver you feel when an incorporeal being passes over you, but has a Ice effect.

Fourteen elements exist in the world. Primary eight: **Fire, Water, Wind, Earth, Thunder, Ice, Dark, Light**. Secondary six: **Plant, Metal, Poison, Spirit, Mind, Ether** (plus Neutral).

Narrative-level facts:
- **Light is not inherently good.** The Cult of Light and Carlon are both Light element. The element represents radiance, revelation, and exposure — which can be used as a weapon.
- **Dark is not inherently evil.** It represents spirits, death, and the unknown — forces that are ancient and neutral, not malevolent.
- **Plant and Spirit** are rare; associated with ancient and primal magic. Ents and the Wooden Rod carry Plant energy.
- **Ether** appears in the Arcmat Ruins context; implies a transcendent or meta-magical force beyond the elemental system.
- **Metal** resists almost everything and is implied to be inert — anti-magical by nature.

## Themes & Tone

- **Cycles of civilization** — Arcmat, the Sundown burial cultures, Verath's academy — all rose and fell. The player walks through layers of history without always knowing it or caring.
- **Nature's indifference** — The Great Roc, the King of the Swamp, the Ents — nature is ancient and powerful and has no interest in human affairs. It will not help you; it will not hate you.
- **The world is older than its inhabitants know** — Every dungeon contains evidence of a prior civilization. The protagonists are not discovering a new world; they are uncovering one they lost.
